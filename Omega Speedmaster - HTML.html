Omega

Speedmaster Moonwatch Profesional

Intro

The Omega Speedmaster is one of the most iconic timepieces in the world, celebrated for its history and rugged design. Known as the “Moonwatch,” it was the first watch worn on the Moon by astronaut Buzz Aldrin in 1969. But it is much more than just a piece of history—this chronograph offers excellent precision, a timeless design, and superb craftsmanship. In this review, we'll explore what makes the Omega Speedmaster such a legendary timepiece and why it's a must-have for watch enthusiasts.

With its signature chronograph functionality and impeccable design, the Speedmaster has earned a place in both horological history and the hearts of collectors.

Design

The design of the Omega Speedmaster is simple yet rugged. Featuring a stainless steel case, a tachymeter bezel, and a clean dial with chronograph subdials, the Speedmaster is both practical and stylish. The watch has a versatile design, making it perfect for both casual wear and formal occasions. Its design is highly legible, with luminous hands and markers that allow for easy reading, even in low-light conditions.

Despite its rugged functionality, the Speedmaster is surprisingly comfortable to wear, with a balanced weight and a solid stainless steel bracelet or leather strap options. The design has stood the test of time, offering a look that feels just as relevant today as it did in the 1960s.

Function Chronograph, Tachymeter, Hour, Minute, and Small Seconds
Jewels 18 jewels

The Omega Speedmaster is powered by the manual-wind Omega Caliber 1861, a movement that is celebrated for its robustness and reliability. The movement is a descendant of the original Caliber 321, used in the early Speedmaster models worn by NASA astronauts during the Apollo missions. The Speedmaster's movement is precise and accurate, with a chronograph function that measures elapsed time, making it a favorite among professionals and enthusiasts alike.

Different Variants (images on right from top to bottom)

The Omega Speedmaster collection includes several variants that cater to different tastes and preferences. Below are some of the standout models:

  • Speedmaster Professional Moonwatch:The most iconic model in the collection, this is the version worn on the Moon. Available with either a Hesalite crystal or sapphire crystal, this classic chronograph features a 42mm stainless steel case and a tachymeter scale bezel. It is an ideal choice for those who appreciate the original design and functionality that has remained unchanged for decades.
  • Speedmaster Reduced: A smaller, 39mm version of the Speedmaster Professional, ideal for those who prefer a slightly more compact and versatile design while retaining the same chronograph functionality. It's an excellent option for individuals with smaller wrists or those who want a more understated look.
  • Speedmaster Dark Side of the Moon: A modern interpretation of the classic Speedmaster, featuring a sleek black ceramic case and a more contemporary design. This variant has a striking, high-tech look and feels at home in both professional and casual settings. Its black ceramic case and contrasting dial make it one of the most visually appealing Speedmaster models.
  • Speedmaster '57: A vintage-inspired version that nods to the original 1957 Speedmaster with a more refined and elegant design. It features a more streamlined case and slightly different subdial configuration, giving it a classic look while integrating modern watchmaking technology.
  • Speedmaster Silver Snoopy Award: A limited-edition model commemorating NASA's Silver Snoopy Award, presented to Omega for its contributions to the Apollo space program. This variant includes unique design elements, such as a playful Snoopy motif on the caseback. The Silver Snoopy is a must-have for collectors looking for something unique and special.
  • Speedmaster Racing:A dynamic and sporty version of the Speedmaster featuring a bold dial design inspired by the world of motorsports. The Speedmaster Racing includes a tachymeter scale and an updated movement for enhanced performance.

Final Verdict

The Omega Speedmaster is a legendary timepiece with a rich history. It's not only a superb chronograph but also a watch with a unique connection to space exploration. Whether you're drawn to its classic design, its connection to the Apollo missions, or its incredible functionality, the Speedmaster is a must-have for any serious watch enthusiast.

In my experience, the Speedmaster Professional Moonwatch is an outstanding watch that combines timeless design, rugged durability, and precise movement. It's definitely a piece that will stand the test of time, both in terms of performance and appeal. If you're looking for a versatile, iconic watch with both style and substance, the Omega Speedmaster is an excellent choice.

It's both attractive and practical, fully meeting my need for a GMT watch. While it's certainly not a Rolex—and doesn't pretend to be— it's a worthwhile alternative for those who want a GMT watch without a sky-high price tag.

I'd recommend this watch to anyone who appreciates the design and functionality of the Rolex GMT Master II but is open to a more affordable option with a few compromises, such as potential movement quirks and a more mainstream look.
